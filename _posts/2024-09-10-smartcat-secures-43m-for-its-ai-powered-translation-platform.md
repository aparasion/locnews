AI is unlikely to fully replace human translators due to its lack of lexical richness and emotional nuance, as it relies on probability rather than lived experience. However, AI translation services are gaining traction among businesses that prioritize accuracy. Smartcat, founded in 2016, offers automated translation tools and has secured over 1,000 corporate clients, including 20% of the Fortune 500. The company operates a platform that matches content with appropriate third-party AI models and provides options for human review, acknowledging AI's occasional errors. Recently, Smartcat raised $43 million in a Series C funding round, bringing its total funding to $70 million, which will be used for team expansion and product development. The global machine translation market is projected to grow significantly, potentially benefiting companies like Smartcat that blend AI with human expertise.
