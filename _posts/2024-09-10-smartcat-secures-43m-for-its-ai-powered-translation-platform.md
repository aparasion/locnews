AI is unlikely to fully replace human translators due to its lack of lexical richness and emotional nuance. While AI can provide accurate translations, they often resemble a textbook version of the original text. Companies like Smartcat, founded in 2016, are capitalizing on the demand for automated translation services, boasting over 1,000 corporate clients, including 20% of the Fortune 500. Smartcat's platform offers a mix of AI and human translation options, allowing clients to choose based on their needs. The company recently raised $43 million in Series C funding, bringing its total to $70 million, which will be used for team expansion and product development. The global machine translation market is projected to grow significantly, indicating a promising future for companies like Smartcat that blend AI with human expertise.
