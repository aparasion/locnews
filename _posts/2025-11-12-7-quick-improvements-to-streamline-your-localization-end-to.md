- Localization leaders face pressure to enhance efficiency and scale operations without significant tech investments. 
- Seven practical improvements can streamline processes and deliver quick wins, including standardized request intake, content tiering, and adopting a machine translation-first approach.
- Implementing a structured intake process can reduce turnaround times, while tiering content allows teams to focus human effort on high-value projects.
- Key performance indicators (KPIs) should align with business objectives to track progress and identify inefficiencies.
- Additional strategies include using modern quality estimation tools and harmonizing vendor discount structures to foster transparency and savings. 

These incremental changes can lead to significant operational improvements and demonstrate localization's value as a cost-saving and growth-driving function.
