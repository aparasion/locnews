Google has announced significant updates to its G Suite tools, enhancing Google Docs with an AI-driven grammar checker, improved spellchecking, and upcoming autocorrect features. These advancements leverage neural network technology to identify grammar issues, similar to machine translation models. Additionally, G Suite users will soon be able to utilize Google Assistant for calendar management, allowing them to create, cancel, and reschedule events via voice commands. This feature, currently in beta, also enables users to send messages to meeting attendees and join calls hands-free. Furthermore, Google is introducing Smart Compose to G Suite, enhancing productivity by streamlining writing tasks. These updates reflect Google’s commitment to integrating AI capabilities into its productivity tools, aiming to improve user experience and efficiency in both document creation and calendar management.
