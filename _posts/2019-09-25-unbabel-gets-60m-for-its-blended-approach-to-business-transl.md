Unbabel, a Lisbon-based startup specializing in scalable customer service translation, has secured $60 million in Series C funding, led by Point72 Ventures. This round increases its total funding to $91 million since its inception in 2013. Unbabel combines machine translation with human expertise, leveraging a network of over 100,000 freelance translators to enhance translation quality and speed. The platform serves more than 150 enterprise clients, including major companies like Facebook and Microsoft, and claims to reduce translation costs by up to 76%. The funding will be used to expand operations in the US, Europe, and Asia, and to enhance its AI capabilities, including establishing a new research lab in Pittsburgh. Co-founder and CEO Vasco Pedro highlighted the growing demand for multilingual customer service, noting that the company now translates over 1 million messages monthly, significantly up from 2018. The investment reflects confidence in Unbabel's potential to transform the translation industry through its innovative technology.
