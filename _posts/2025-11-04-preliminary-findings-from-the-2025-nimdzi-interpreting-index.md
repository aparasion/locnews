- The 2025 Nimdzi Interpreting Index projects the interpreting sector will reach USD 11.7 billion in 2024, growing to USD 17.2 billion by 2029, driven by an 8% CAGR, despite potential government budget cuts impacting demand.
- The interpreting industry is increasingly influenced by geopolitical and economic factors, with rising demand during crises but facing challenges from U.S. policies reducing federal multilingual funding.
- AI is being integrated into the interpreting field, primarily in low-stakes scenarios, enhancing efficiency but not replacing human interpreters, who remain essential for high-value communications.
- A significant shortage of qualified interpreters is emerging due to declining enrollment in language programs and rising living costs, posing a threat to industry growth unless addressed through investment in education and professional development.
- Recent mergers and acquisitions, particularly in the U.S. and Europe, are reshaping the competitive landscape, with companies seeking to expand services and adapt to technological advancements in AI.
