- The 2025 Nimdzi Brand Awareness Study analyzes brand perception in the language services market, drawing insights from 271 technology-forward buyers across five continents, primarily North America and Europe.
- The study highlights a shift towards valuing automation, platform integration, and measurable outcomes over traditional service relationships, indicating a transformation in buyer preferences driven by digital advancements.
- Key metrics such as unaided and top-of-mind awareness reveal which language service providers dominate buyer consciousness, with 39 companies mentioned as top-of-mind, but only 13 receiving significant recognition.
- The findings suggest that successful vendors are those adapting to the evolving market, while those clinging to legacy models may struggle as procurement frameworks from tech sectors influence other industries.
- The study emphasizes the importance of brand visibility and strategic positioning for language service providers aiming for growth in a competitive landscape, with insights serving as a guide for decision-making in 2026 and beyond.
