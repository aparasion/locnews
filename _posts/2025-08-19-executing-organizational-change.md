- **Execution Focus**: Successful organizational change hinges on effective execution of strategic positioning, requiring tailored plans for centralization, decentralization, or distribution models.
  
- **Phased Implementation**: Each approach involves distinct phases—assessment, consolidation, optimization for centralization; assessment, empowerment, coordination for decentralization; and design, implementation, optimization for distributed models.

- **Cultural Considerations**: Centralization and decentralization are not just operational shifts but cultural transformations that necessitate understanding stakeholder needs and fostering relationships.

- **Real-World Examples**: Case studies illustrate the importance of adapting strategies to organizational realities, emphasizing that successful localization requires aligning with broader business goals rather than just localization-specific metrics.

- **Strategic Value**: Localization teams can leverage organizational changes to demonstrate their value, transforming from reactive to proactive contributors in driving business success.
