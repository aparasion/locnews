Personal translation devices have evolved significantly over the past decade, driven by advancements in processing power, machine learning, and cloud technology. As these devices become integrated into wireless earbuds and generative AI platforms, real-time smartphone translation is expected to become commonplace. This shift poses challenges for companies focused on standalone translation devices. 

At CES 2024, Timekettle unveiled the X1 Interpreter Hub, touted as the world's first multi-language simultaneous interpretation system. Designed for meetings, the X1 allows two devices to connect for instant translation, supporting up to 20 users in five languages without the need for a separate app. It also accommodates virtual conversations via a dedicated phone number. Priced at $699, the X1 represents a significant step in making translation technology more accessible and efficient, potentially reshaping communication in diverse settings.
