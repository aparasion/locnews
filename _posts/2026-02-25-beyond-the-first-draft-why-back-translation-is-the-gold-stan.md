- In 2026, the focus for global enterprises shifts from merely translating documents to ensuring their accuracy, particularly in high-stakes sectors like pharmaceuticals and legal fields.
- Back-Translation is introduced as a crucial quality assurance process, involving an independent linguist translating a document back to its original language to identify discrepancies in meaning and nuance.
- Relying solely on forward-only translations poses risks such as semantic shifts, cultural misunderstandings, and compliance failures with regulatory bodies like the FDA and EMA.
- Day Translations employs a rigorous, ISO-compliant workflow that includes forward translation, blinded back-translation, reconciliation, and final validation to ensure linguistic accuracy.
- Back-Translation is essential for critical documents like clinical trial protocols and informed consent forms, emphasizing the need for precise communication in regulated environments.
